Someone brought up a feature request for the info window, and it makes
me realize that there are two possible paths Sonata could go, but not
both.

Currently, Sonata is set up so that when you open the info window, it
stays synced with the currently playing song. What this means is that
you can click play, open the info window, and then click next song,
the info window will update to this next song. On the other hand, gmpc
works differently, which is to say that the user must manually dictate
which song(s) to display. So if you press play in gmpc, open the info
window, and then press next song, the info window won't automatically
switch to the current song.

Both paths have their benefits. In the current Sonata implementation,
it's very nice for the user who prefers to have updated/synced
information displayed as they listen to their playlist. On the gmpc
side though, you are able to click on any song in your
library/playlist and obtain information about it. The two seem pretty
much mutually exclusive.

Anyone have a strong opinion either way? I'm tempted to continue with
the current approach, but maybe I'm in the minority.
