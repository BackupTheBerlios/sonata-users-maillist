>> 2. The new "Info" tab

I feel a bit mitigated about this.

On the one hand, using one of my personal boxes with a nice tiling
window manager such as XMonad, I preferred the good old info window:
I could fine-tune how/where it was shown or hidden, and moreover, the
way to select this window was consistent with my whole WM behaviour,
instead of the new « Select Sonata main window, then Alt-Shift-3
(using a non-qwerty keyboard, with shifted numbers) ».

On the other hand, using a random box with Gnome, KDE or whatever
desktop environment running a window manager I can’t stand, I prefer
the new info tab: in this situation, the info window was Yet Another
Window, poorly managed by the WM, getting in my way when pressing
Alt-TAB.

To sum up: it seems software developers sometimes tend to try and
manage themselves their piece of software’s windows, instead of
letting the WM do it. This workaround is quite understandable, seeing
how most WM do a poor job managing windows (sure, they make a good job
decorating windows, but hey, they’re supposed to be window managers,
not window decorators, after all). However, this brings tons of
inconsistencies on the desktop: different pieces of software implement
their own tabs or whatever, along with their own navigation
keybindings (CTRL-PageUp/PageDown, Alt-1/2..., etc.), in place of
separate windows, in situations such as a separate window would maybe
make more sense.
