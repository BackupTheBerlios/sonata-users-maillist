Yes, thanks Scott, though don't you mean genre and artist?
Both are okay but neither behaves like QL's Album List view.
For example, artist view is of limited value in classical since it
eventually leads me to everything conducted by, say, Karajan.
(Hopefully Sonata will soon recognise the Composer tag).
Genre view has similar limitations. I click on, say, "Opera" and
instead of seeing all the operas in my collection, I'm presented with
a large list of people(!), and all of them, conductors only. And so
on...
In all cases, with Sonata, one has to drill down and down and, in
particular, know exactly where or what something is ahead of time.
With a collection of significant depth and breadth this is arduous, to
say the least.

The beauty of QL's Album List view, on the other hand, is that it's
just that: a simple top-level list of all ones albums, irrespective of
artist, composer, genre, or location within a file system hierarchy.
It makes browsing a simple "see it, play it" experience.

Hope this clarifies.
-Fergus

PS: I'm using Sonata 1.5.3, svn r. 896
