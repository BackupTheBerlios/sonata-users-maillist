On Wed, Sep 10, 2008 at 7:54 PM, alkos333 <me at alkos333.net> wrote:
> I believe you it happened.  Why would it be terrible for a client to
> keep the id of the next song to be played?

Because the whole idea of the client-server design is for the client
to be stateless. I sometimes have ncmpc and Sonata both open, and
ncmpc would get completely lost if Sonata started queing things
locally it didn't know about. This isn't even mentioning the easy "mpc
pause" and "mpc next" commands I may have assigned to icons or
keyboard shortcuts.

Sonata is not a music player. It is not a music organizer. it is
simply a frontend to mpd, so for it to do things that mpd cannot would
be outside its role.

-Dan
