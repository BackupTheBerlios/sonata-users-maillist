Hi,

Firstly, thanks for such a beautiful music player :)

As someone who, by habit, doesn't use playlists, but manually adds chosen
songs now and again, I have a feature request.

I was until this week using Sonata 1.4 from Ubuntu 8.04. It allowed the
ability to choose whether or not to automatically play a song after
double-clicking and adding it from the library. I like this behaviour, but
obviously it is not desirable all of the time. Since updating to Sonata 1.5
I see that this option has been replaced with the "Play after" sub-menu to
fix this.

In my opinion it would be in keeping with Sonata's simplicity to remove this
"Play-after" submenu, and change the behaviour back to auto-play the song
just added, *but* only if there is no song currently playing. Other music
players work like this also.

This is the way I have patched my Sonata, and I'm extremely happy with it. I
thought I would raise the idea here, to see what people think of the
suggestion.

Matthew.

in sonata/main.py to: if play_after and self.status['state'] in ['pause',
'stop']:
