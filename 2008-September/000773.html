On Mon, Sep 29, 2008 at 6:25 AM, J.M. Peng Hardin
<peng.thinkblue at gmail.com>wrote:

>  I love Sonata, but searching for tunes gets really tough when I have
> multiple versions of a song. Sometimes I'll have a studio version (or two),
> a live version, and more than one version in a compilation. Would it be
> possible to get a tooltip on the search results to let me hover my cursor on
> the track and get the album it's on or at least include the album name in
> the visible results?
>

I was just looking into a similar request (see
https://developer.berlios.de/feature/?func=detailfeature&feature_id=3905&group_id=7323)
last night.

It seems that the best three options, while preserving simplicity when there
aren't multiple versions, are:
 1. Show album/path for duplicates songs only.
 2. Allow toggling on and off albums/paths for all songs.
 3. Show albums/paths in tooltips.

Certainly 3 is the least obtrusive, but also the most manual work (to hover
over each element). Anyone care enough to have a preference? I'm tempted to
go 1 or 2 and I'm leaning towards 1, even if it means potentially missing
duplicates because of misspellings (as mentioned in the feature request).

Scott
