On Wed, Jun 17, 2009 at 2:30 AM, Stephen Boyd <bebarino at gmail.com> wrote:

> Instead of having a popup dialog ask you to either replace or append to
> the selected playlist, provide two popup menus for the replace and
> append actions. This should be less intrusive.
>
> Allow the user to append and replace songs from within the library tab
> in addition to the usual current tab. This makes it easier to build
> "misc" playlists without requiring you to add songs to the current
> tab first.
>

I had a slight qualm about changing from the default of the entire list to
only the selection, but it is clearly labeled and certainly more powerful,
so it's probably a big improvement. And being able to perform the action
from the library is certainly a nice bonus!

And while I know that nobody likes popups, I'm not sure anybody really likes
additional levels in the menu either, nor the repetitiveness of the two
submenus. I toyed around with the idea that people might generally just
perform appends or replaces, and not both, and that we should make it a
preference (or alternatively a checkbox on the window that says 'always
perform this action'). This would be simplest and cleanest assuming my
assumption is correct. But I go back and forth on all of this, and frankly I
don't even use playlists.

Any chance you can split this patch into 2 patches and apply the selection
piece for the time being?

Scott
