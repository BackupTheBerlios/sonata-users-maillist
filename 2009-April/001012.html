On Sun, Apr 5, 2009 at 8:28 PM, Dan McGee <dpmcgee at gmail.com> wrote:

> Because up until this point I have never had to, and I like to have
> multiple applications tiled at once. I tend to run an IM client, an IM
> window, and sonata all on the same screen, and even at 800x480 Sonata
> is very usable only using 1/3 or so of it.
>

Indeed, Sonata should be kept very usable by people (like me) that keep it
small. From what I've observed when looking at screenshots, it seems that
the majority of people keep the window in a smaller (non-maximized) state.

I originally made the icons in the filesystem view larger for consistency
with the other views - aesthetically, this was more important in version 1.6
because all of the views shared the ".." and "/" views.

However, I've come to realize that while I do a lot of switching library
views (often, but not exclusively, for testing), I can imagine most users
may always stick to one view. In fact, if you're using the filesystem view,
I'd wager that you might never use any of the other views. That combined
with us removing the ".." and "/" list items (I would really like to see
Sonata use the breadcrumbs approach that Tuukka has been working on) makes
the larger size icons much less compelling. So I just reverted the behavior
for the Filesystem view, they now use the smaller size.
