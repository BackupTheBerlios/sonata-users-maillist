Hi,

Sonata is very cool.

There is one minor issue. GTK has a dumb "feature" where if a combo
box is at the bottom of the screen, you have to scroll a bit to see
any of the items in the list. This shows up in sonata when you use the
library browser. If the sonata window is fullscreen or near the bottom
of the screen, then libraryview button and searchcombo combo box will
want to go off the edge of the screen and thus scrolling will happen.
Its a very minor annoyance, and most of you probably haven't noticed,
but I use a tiling window manager so it almost always happens.

There is a simple way to fix it, so simple I won't even submit a
patch: just swap lines 639 and 640 in sonata.py. You can try it on
your current installation but just going to
/usr/share/pycentral/sonata/site-packages/sonata.py and making the
change there. (Or where ever it's installed. This is where
debian/ubuntu puts it.) It just changes the packing order of the
widgets. I think the search bar looks just fine up at the top, and it
avoids that annoying GTK bug because there's always room for the menus
to open downward.

I've made the change on my local installation, so I don't really need
a patch. I just thought I'd bring it to your attention.

Thanks again for the cool client.

Scott
